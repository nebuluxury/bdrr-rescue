Home The Beagles

2,110 beagles. One shot at freedom.

They were bred for laboratories and had never touched grass. Then Big Dog Ranch Rescue stepped in, in one of the largest beagle rescues in history.

What Happened

From cages to couches.

Ridglan Farms in Wisconsin bred beagles for animal testing, roughly 2,000 dogs living in cages, many having never known a walk, a toy, or a name.

After the facility agreed to surrender its license, Big Dog Ranch Rescue partnered with the Center for a Humane Economy to purchase and rescue the dogs, transporting them across the country to safety, veterinary care, and, finally, foster and forever homes.

"It's a very big win, and I am ecstatic to have these dogs out and get them into loving homes." — Lauree Simmons, Founder

How It Unfolded

The rescue, step by step

October 2025

The facility agrees to close

Facing felony animal-mistreatment charges, Ridglan Farms agrees to surrender its breeding license, effective July 2026.

April 29, 2026

The deal to free them

Big Dog Ranch Rescue and the Center for a Humane Economy announce an agreement to purchase nearly 1,500 beagles.

May 7, 2026

The first 1,000 come home

BDRR completes the removal of the first 1,000 beagles, loading them onto transports bound for safety.

June 2, 2026

135 more freed

Another group is secured, with beagles arriving at the Florida campus to begin their new lives.

Summer 2026

The doors close for good

The remaining beagles are placed and the facility shuts down permanently, bringing BDRR's total to 2,110 dogs saved.

Why Beagles

Gentle by nature, chosen by labs

Beagles are used in testing precisely because they are so trusting and forgiving. These dogs deserved better, and now they have it.
