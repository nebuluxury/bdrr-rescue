Until every dog has a home.

Our Story

It started with 14 dogs.

In 2008, founder Lauree Simmons walked into a kill shelter and left with 14 dogs slated for euthanasia. That single afternoon became Big Dog Ranch Rescue.

Today, our 33-acre cage-free ranch in Loxahatchee, Florida and our 100-acre campus in Alabama care for more than 1,600 dogs at a time, and we have saved over 85,000 lives and counting.

"Our mission is to rescue, rehabilitate, and educate until every dog has a loving and safe place to call home."

How We'll End Dog Homelessness

Four pillars, one mission

Education

Raising awareness about the pet overpopulation crisis and responsible ownership.

Sterilization

Free and low-cost spay/neuter to stop the cycle of homelessness at its source.

Legislation

Advocating for stronger protections for dogs and the shelters that serve them.

Collaboration

Uniting rescues, shelters, and animal lovers into one powerful force for good.
