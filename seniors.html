Home Senior Dogs

It's never too late for a new beginning.

Senior dogs wait the longest and love the hardest. They are calm, house-trained, and endlessly grateful for the gift of a home.

Why Adopt a Senior

What you see is what you get.

With a senior dog, there are no surprises. Their size, personality, and temperament are all settled, so you can find the perfect fit for your life on day one.

Most are already house-trained and know basic commands, which means you skip the chewing, the crying, and the 5am puppy zoomies, and go straight to the good part: companionship.

Meet Adoptable Seniors
Myth vs. Truth

Let's clear a few things up

The reasons people hesitate to adopt older dogs are usually based on myths. Here's the reality.

The Myth

"Old dogs have behavior problems."

The Truth

Most seniors are surrendered because of a human life change, a death, a move, an illness, not because of anything they did.

The Myth

"You can't teach an old dog new tricks."

The Truth

Older dogs are focused, eager to please, and learn quickly, often faster than a distractible puppy.

The Myth

"It's too sad, they don't have long."

The Truth

Many seniors have years of love left, and giving them a wonderful final chapter is one of life's most rewarding gifts.

Our Programs

Seniors for Seniors & Fospice

Seniors for Seniors pairs older adopters with older dogs, a gentle match of two hearts at the same easy pace, often with reduced adoption fees.

Fospice (foster + hospice) surrounds our most senior dogs with comfort, love, and full veterinary support in a real home for their final chapter. No dog spends their last days alone.
